Who we are

Where the forest
meets the algorithm

A(i)mazon is an international, multi-disciplinary research collective united by a single ambition: understanding and preserving the Amazon rainforest using the most advanced computational tools available.

We bring together biologists, ecologists, computer scientists, and data scientists from leading universities across the UK and Brazil. Our work spans from ground-level species surveys to satellite-derived canopy mapping — always asking how artificial intelligence can sharpen our view of the most biodiverse place on Earth.

From LiDAR point-cloud segmentation to deep-learning-based tree species recognition, we develop open, reproducible methods that the broader scientific community can build upon.

Research Themes

What we work on

🌿

Tree Species Recognition

We develop deep learning pipelines to identify and classify Amazonian tree species from aerial and satellite imagery, LiDAR waveforms, and hyperspectral sensors — pushing toward automated biodiversity inventories at scale.

Computer Vision · Taxonomy
📡

LiDAR Forest Segmentation

Individual Tree Crown (ITC) delineation from airborne LiDAR point clouds using geometric and machine learning approaches. We map canopy structure to infer forest health, biomass, and structural diversity.

Point Clouds · 3D Sensing
🛰️

Remote Sensing & Change Detection

Time-series analysis of multispectral and SAR satellite data to detect deforestation, forest degradation, and phenological shifts across the Brazilian Amazon at regional and continental scales.

Sentinel · PALSAR · MODIS
🧬

Biodiversity Modelling

Integrating field-collected ecological data with AI-derived remote sensing products to model species distribution, alpha and beta diversity, and functional trait variation across the Amazon basin.

SDM · Trait Ecology
🤖

Foundation Models for Ecology

Adapting and fine-tuning large vision and language models (ViTs, SAM, CLIP) on Amazonian ecological datasets, reducing the need for large labelled training sets in data-scarce tropical environments.

SAM · ViT · Transfer Learning
📊

Open Data & Reproducibility

We are committed to open science. We publish benchmark datasets, annotated LiDAR collections, and reusable model checkpoints to accelerate tropical forest research globally.

Benchmarks · Open Source
