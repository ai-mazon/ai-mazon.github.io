// About

Decoding biodiversity
one tree at a time

A(i)mazon is an international, interdisciplinary research group bringing together biologists, ecologists, computer scientists, and data scientists from leading universities across the UK and Brazil.

We develop and apply state-of-the-art AI and machine learning methods to understand, map, and protect the Amazon rainforest — the most biodiverse ecosystem on Earth. From airborne LiDAR to deep learning models trained on millions of tree observations, our work pushes the frontier of what's possible at the intersection of ecology and technology.

What we work on

Tree Species Recognition

Deep learning models for automated identification of Amazonian tree species from aerial imagery, hyperspectral data, and field observations.

  • CNN
  • Hyperspectral
  • Classification

LiDAR Segmentation

Novel algorithms for individual tree segmentation and structural analysis from airborne and terrestrial LiDAR point clouds in dense tropical canopies.

  • Point Clouds
  • 3D Segmentation
  • Canopy

Remote Sensing & Mapping

Large-scale biodiversity and forest structure mapping using satellite and airborne sensors, integrating multi-modal data fusion approaches.

  • Satellite
  • Data Fusion
  • Mapping

Biodiversity Modelling

Statistical and machine learning models relating forest structure, climate, and species diversity across Amazonian landscapes and climate gradients.

  • Species Distribution
  • Ecology
  • ML Models

Forest Carbon & Climate

Quantifying above-ground biomass and carbon stocks to support climate science, REDD+ monitoring, and deforestation detection at scale.

  • Biomass
  • Carbon
  • REDD+

Open Tools & Datasets

Building open-source pipelines, benchmarks, and annotated datasets to accelerate the broader remote sensing and tropical ecology communities.

  • Open Source
  • Benchmarks
  • Data
